What is Sartre's 'Facticity'?

In existentialism, facticity refers to the objective facts of a person's life that they cannot change, such as their birth, physical characteristics, environment, and past choices. It is the "given" context within which human freedom must operate, representing the resistance of reality against absolute freedom.
